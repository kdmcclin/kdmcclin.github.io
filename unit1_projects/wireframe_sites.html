Wireframing


Pandora solves the problem of introducing you to new music based on a category that you already enjoy. It uses the Music Genome Project to stream similar songs based on your initial input. It faces the visual challenge of expanding or contracting depending on the number of stations a user has made, as well as the challenge of changing photos depending on the album being played. Along with this latter challenge is the subchallenge of maintaining a brief visual history of the last few albums played.

Nationals.com solves the problem of disseminating information that is anyway related to the team or MLB in general. It faces the visual challenge of presenting a great deal of information in a way that does not overwhelm the user.

Tumblr solves the problem of bringing together people and topics in the same place without the more life-invasive quality of Facebook, the brevity of Twitter, and the isolated nature of traditional blogs. It faces the visual challenge of a dashboard with infinite scroll depending on how far the useer wants to scroll down and the possibility/likelihood of image heavy posts (particularly with gifsets) that take time to load.
